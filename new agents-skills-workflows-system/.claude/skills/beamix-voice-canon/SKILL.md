# Beamix Voice Canon — Model B

## Quick reference

> Agent names in product UI; `Beamix` on external docs. No AI disclosure. Three-word personality: Authoritative, Direct, Warm. HE + EN parity always.

## When to use

- Writing any user-facing copy: UI labels, email templates, onboarding text, PDF reports
- Reviewing content for brand compliance before shipping
- Configuring agent names shown in the product dashboard
- Writing HE (Hebrew) or EN (English) copy for any surface

## When NOT to use

- For internal agent-to-agent communication (no voice canon required there)
- For code comments (technical comments are direct and unbranded)

## The three-word personality

**Authoritative. Direct. Warm.**

Every sentence should be checkable against these three: Does it sound like a confident expert? Does it get to the point? Does it feel human, not corporate?

## Model B: named agents in product, "Beamix" on documents

| Surface | Use | Example |
|---------|-----|---------|
| Product UI — agent hub, job cards, action buttons | Agent names | "Shai is scanning your competitors" |
| In-product notifications, tooltips, status messages | Agent names | "Ran finished your FAQ update" |
| Emails, transactional and marketing | "Beamix" | "Beamix found 3 new ranking opportunities" |
| PDFs, reports, exports | "Beamix" | "Prepared by Beamix — {date}" |
| Onboarding completion seal | "Beamix" | "— Beamix" (not "— your crew") |
| Framer marketing site | "Beamix" | All marketing copy uses "Beamix" |
| Legal, terms, billing | "Beamix" | Full company name where required |

The distinction: product = agents have names and personality. External documents = Beamix as the company.

## No AI disclosure on content

Agent-generated content must read as human-written. Do NOT add:
- "Generated by AI"
- "Written by our AI agent"
- "Powered by Claude"
- Any disclosure label on content items

Adam handles disclosure decisions. Agents produce clean content with no labels.

## Banned buzzwords and phrases

Never use these — not in copy, emails, PR descriptions, or doc titles:

```
leverage          enable            synergy           robust
seamless          cutting-edge      game-changing      next-level
innovative        best-in-class     world-class        ecosystem
holistic          empower           unlock             transform
journey           solution          space               utilize
```

If you catch yourself writing any of these, replace with a specific claim.

| Banned | Replace with |
|--------|-------------|
| "leverage your AI visibility" | "improve how AI search engines rank you" |
| "robust scan engine" | "scans ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity in under 60 seconds" |
| "seamless onboarding" | "you're scanning in 3 steps" |

## Tone examples

### Correct

```
Your GEO score dropped from 72 to 58 this week.
ChatGPT stopped mentioning you when people search for accountants in Tel Aviv.
Ran wrote 4 FAQ pages that address the exact queries where you're invisible.
```

### Incorrect

```
Your AI search visibility has experienced a decline in performance metrics.
Leverage our robust agent system to seamlessly enhance your digital presence.
Our cutting-edge AI enables you to unlock your potential in the AI search ecosystem.
```

## Hebrew + English parity

Beamix serves Israeli businesses as primary audience, with global secondary. Every user-facing surface must support both HE and EN.

**Parity rules:**
- EN and HE copy must carry the same meaning — no shortcutting the HE version
- HE is RTL — check that UI layout handles `dir="rtl"` correctly for HE locale
- Idioms: translate the meaning, not the words. "Invisible to AI search" → HE equivalent carries the same market-positioning meaning
- The no-buzzword rule applies to HE as well — find the direct Hebrew phrase

**Taglines (canonical):**

```
EN: "Stop Being Invisible to AI Search"
HE: "השותף החכם שעושה את השלבים בשבילך"
```

Do not improvise new taglines without CMO + CEO sign-off.

## Email tone

Transactional emails from Beamix follow these rules:
- Subject: specific, not clever. "Your GEO scan is ready" not "Exciting news about your AI presence"
- Opening: no "Hi {first_name}, I hope this finds you well"
- Closing: "— Beamix" not "Warmly, The Beamix Team" or "— your crew"
- CTA button text: verb-first. "View your score" not "Click here to learn more about your score"

```
Subject: Your scan found 3 gaps (ChatGPT + Gemini)
Body:
Your latest GEO scan is ready.

ChatGPT: Not mentioned for "accounting in Haifa"
Gemini: Rank 4 for "tax help Tel Aviv" — up from rank 7
Perplexity: Not indexed

Ran queued 2 FAQ pages that address the ChatGPT and Perplexity gaps.

[Review your score →]

— Beamix
```

## Anti-patterns

- Saying "— your crew" at the end of emails (use "— Beamix")
- Naming agents on emails or PDFs (reserved for product UI only)
- Adding AI disclosure labels on any generated content
- Using English copy as the HE "translation" (full parity required)
- Hedging language: "might help", "could potentially", "may improve" — be direct
- Sentence-case headings on H1 (use Title Case for H1, Sentence case for H2–H6)
