## North Star Metric

> **How to choose your North Star — Stage guidance (delete this block after you've filled in your metric):**
> - **Pre-PMF:** Choose an engagement or activation metric — something that shows users are getting value. e.g., "Users who return 3+ times in their first week", "Projects created", "Tasks completed."
> - **Post-PMF:** Shift to a growth and retention metric. e.g., "Weekly Active Users", "Items processed per month."
> - **Scale stage:** Use a revenue-correlated metric. e.g., "ARR per cohort-month", "Paying customers who expand."
>
> **Anti-patterns — avoid these as your North Star:**
> - Revenue or MRR (an outcome, not a value signal — gaming it doesn't mean users got value)
> - DAU / MAU (too broad — includes users who logged in but did nothing meaningful)
> - Page views (not correlated with value delivery)
> - Signups (a vanity metric — activation is what matters)
>
> **Validity test:** Can every team trace their work back to moving this metric? If the answer for some teams is "no," it's not the right North Star.

## Metric Lifecycle & Review Cadence

> Most startups change their North Star once or twice before PMF — that is expected. Document any change in `.claude/memory/DECISIONS.md` with the reason.

**North Star Validity Checklist (review quarterly):**
- [ ] Is it customer-value-correlated? (When this goes up, users are genuinely better off)
- [ ] Is it leadable? (Can the team take direct actions to move it?)
- [ ] Can every team trace their work to it?
- [ ] Is it specific enough to be gamed in a meaningful way? (Gaming it should require actually helping users)
- [ ] Has it held as the best signal for 2+ months?

**Review Cadence**

| Cadence | What to Review | Owner | Forum |
|---------|---------------|-------|-------|
| **Weekly** | Current value vs. target; trend direction (up/flat/down) | data-lead | Weekly metrics review |
| **Monthly** | OKR progress; which input metrics moved the North Star | ceo + business-lead | Monthly business review |
| **Quarterly** | Is the North Star still the right metric for our current stage? | ceo | Quarterly planning |

## Leading vs. Lagging Indicators

> **Leading indicators** are input metrics — things the team can directly control that, when improved, eventually move the North Star. **Lagging indicators** are output metrics — they confirm results but arrive too late to course-correct in-quarter.
>
> Rule: If you only track lagging indicators, you find out you missed the quarter after the quarter ends. Track leading indicators weekly; lagging indicators monthly.

| Leading Indicator (Input — control this) | Lagging Indicator (Output — this confirms it worked) |
